  1. Corrected distance visual acuity (CDVA) is 6/18 or better prior to the treatment.
  2. Where progressive keratoconus is demonstrated by at least one of the following:
    • a measured increase in maximum central Amsler-Krumeich reading (K value) of at least 1.00 D over a maximum 2 year period
    • a change in either myopia and/or astigmatism of 1 diopters (D) or more over a period of 1 year, or
    • a mean central corneal thickness decrease of 5% or more over a period of 1 year.
Corneal crosslinking will not be reimbursed in the following circumstances:
  • for patients where the disease is stable
  • where the corneal thickness is measured at less than 400 microns
  • for patients with a Amsler-Krumeich reading (K value) over 58.00 D
  • for patients over the age of 35 years
  • for patients with visually significant corneal opacity
  • for patients with history of uveitis or herpes simplex keratitis
  • for patients with a documented history of delayed wound healing
  • for treatment of post-LASIK keratectasia.
